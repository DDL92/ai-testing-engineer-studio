# CRM Usage Guide

Use `leads.csv` as the single source of truth for prospects, outreach, follow-ups, and deal status.

## How To Add Leads

1. Add one row per lead.
2. Include platform, company, role, source, service fit, and pain point.
3. Assign priority: High, Medium, or Low.
4. Set status to `New` or `Qualified`.
5. Add the next follow-up date before ending the session.

## How To Qualify Leads

A lead is qualified when:

- The company has a SaaS, AI app, agency, or web product.
- A clear QA pain exists.
- One of the four services maps to the pain.
- The lead has enough context to personalize outreach.

## How To Update Status

Update status immediately after every action:

- After sending a message, set `Contacted`.
- After a reply, set `Replied`.
- If they want details, set `Interested`.
- After sending pricing/scope, set `Proposal Sent`.
- After payment or acceptance, set `Won`.

## How To Schedule Follow-Ups

- First follow-up: 2 days after outreach.
- Second follow-up: 5 days after outreach.
- Value-add follow-up: 10 days after outreach.
- Stop after the final follow-up unless the lead replies.

## Daily CRM Use

1. Filter leads with follow-ups due today.
2. Send follow-ups first.
3. Add 5 new leads.
4. Send 3-5 personalized messages.
5. Update status, notes, and next follow-up date.

## Weekly CRM Review

Every Friday, count:

- New leads.
- Qualified leads.
- Messages sent.
- Replies.
- Calls booked.
- Proposals sent.
- Deals won/lost.
- Follow-ups due next week.

## Example Workflow: New To Won

1. `New`: Find SaaS founder on LinkedIn.
2. `Qualified`: Confirm they ship a web app and mention release pain.
3. `Contacted`: Send short audit message.
4. `Replied`: They ask for details.
5. `Interested`: Send audit outline and ask scope questions.
6. `Proposal Sent`: Send $500 Playwright QA Automation Audit proposal.
7. `Won`: Client accepts and pays.
