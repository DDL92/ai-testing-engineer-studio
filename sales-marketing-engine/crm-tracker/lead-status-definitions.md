# Lead Status Definitions

## New

Meaning: Lead was added but not researched deeply.  
When to use it: Initial capture from LinkedIn, Upwork, email, or referral.  
Next action: Research and qualify.  
Example note: "SaaS founder, possible dashboard regression pain."

## Qualified

Meaning: Lead appears to match one service and has a clear pain signal.  
When to use it: After confirming product type, role, and pain.  
Next action: Send personalized outreach.  
Example note: "Mentions manual QA and upcoming release."

## Contacted

Meaning: First message, email, or proposal was sent.  
When to use it: Immediately after outreach.  
Next action: Schedule Day 2 follow-up.  
Example note: "Sent Playwright audit outline."

## Replied

Meaning: Lead responded but has not clearly shown buying intent.  
When to use it: Any non-negative reply.  
Next action: Answer question and qualify need.  
Example note: "Asked whether audit includes starter tests."

## Interested

Meaning: Lead is actively considering scope, price, or call.  
When to use it: They ask for details, examples, pricing, or timeline.  
Next action: Send proposal or book call.  
Example note: "Requested price for framework setup."

## Call Booked

Meaning: Discovery or scope call is scheduled.  
When to use it: Calendar time is confirmed.  
Next action: Prepare questions and proposal angle.  
Example note: "Call Friday, discuss AI response quality audit."

## Proposal Sent

Meaning: Formal scope, price, and deliverables were sent.  
When to use it: After sending a fixed-scope proposal.  
Next action: Follow up after 2 days.  
Example note: "$500 audit proposal sent, 3-day timeline."

## Won

Meaning: Client accepted and payment or contract is confirmed.  
When to use it: Deal is closed.  
Next action: Start delivery and update revenue dashboard.  
Example note: "Paid Playwright audit, delivery starts Monday."

## Lost

Meaning: Lead declined or selected another option.  
When to use it: Clear no, no budget, or bad fit.  
Next action: Record lesson and stop follow-up.  
Example note: "Wanted full-time manual QA only."

## Follow Up Later

Meaning: Lead is relevant but timing is not right.  
When to use it: They ask to revisit later or project is delayed.  
Next action: Set future date and add context.  
Example note: "Revisit after product launch next month."
