# Objection Handling

## Too Expensive

Best response: We can reduce the first scope instead of discounting the full outcome.  
What to offer: Smaller audit around 2-3 flows.  
What not to say: "I can do it cheaper."  
Follow-up CTA: Want me to send the smaller audit scope?

## Not Needed Now

Best response: Understood. The audit is most useful before releases or when manual regression slows the team.  
What to offer: Follow up later.  
What not to say: "You really need this."  
Follow-up CTA: Should I check back next month?

## We Already Have Automation

Best response: Great. I can focus on gaps, flaky tests, and CI reliability instead of starting over.  
What to offer: Automation health audit.  
What not to say: "Your current setup is probably bad."  
Follow-up CTA: Want me to review the current test strategy?

## We Use Cypress/Selenium

Best response: That can still work. I can audit the current coverage and recommend whether Playwright migration is worth it.  
What to offer: Tool-agnostic QA automation audit.  
What not to say: "Playwright is always better."  
Follow-up CTA: Want a comparison-focused audit?

## We Do Not Have Test Cases

Best response: That is common. I can derive smoke scenarios from critical flows and recent bugs.  
What to offer: Audit plus scenario design.  
What not to say: "You need documentation first."  
Follow-up CTA: Send the top three flows and I can map the first tests.

## We Do Not Have Staging

Best response: We can still audit public flows and define automation readiness, but implementation may be limited.  
What to offer: Readiness audit.  
What not to say: "I cannot help."  
Follow-up CTA: Want a readiness-first audit?

## We Have No QA Process

Best response: Then the first step should be simple: critical flows, risks, and starter smoke coverage.  
What to offer: Playwright QA Automation Audit.  
What not to say: "You need a full QA strategy."  
Follow-up CTA: Want the audit outline?

## We Need Full-Time Only

Best response: Understood. I am best for fixed-scope audits, frameworks, and retainers.  
What to offer: Contractor support until full-time hire.  
What not to say: "You should hire me instead."  
Follow-up CTA: Need interim QA automation support?

## We Need Someone Cheaper

Best response: I understand. My work is best when quality, maintainability, and release confidence matter.  
What to offer: Smaller scope.  
What not to say: "Cheap work is bad."  
Follow-up CTA: Want a reduced first milestone?
